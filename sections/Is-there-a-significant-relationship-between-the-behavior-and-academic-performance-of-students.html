Is there a significant relationship between the behavior and academic performance of students?

Mobile games refer to video games that are designed to be played on mobile devices such as smartphones and tablets. These games come in various genres, including action, adventure, puzzle, strategy, and simulation, among others. They are easily accessible, affordable, and can be played on-the-go, making them popular among people of all ages.

  • Candy Crush Saga: This is a popular puzzle game that involves matching candies of the same color to progress through levels.
  • Clash of Clans: This is a strategy game that involves building a village, training troops, and battling against other players.
  • PUBG Mobile: This is a multiplayer battle royale game that involves players fighting against each other to be the last one standing.
  • Among Us: This is a social deduction game that involves players working together to complete tasks on a spaceship while trying to identify and vote off any imposters among them.
  • Pokemon Go: This is an augmented reality game that involves players exploring real-world locations to catch virtual creatures called Pokemon.

Relationship between Mobile Game Behavior and Academic Performance:

There is some research that suggests a significant relationship between mobile game behavior and academic performance among students. Some studies have found that excessive mobile game use can lead to poor academic performance, as it may lead to distractions, reduced study time, and poor time management skills. Additionally, mobile games can be addictive, and students may spend more time playing games than studying or completing academic tasks. However, it's important to note that not all students who play mobile games will have poor academic performance, and many factors can impact academic success.
